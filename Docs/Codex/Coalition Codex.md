## 1. Definition

A **Coalition** is a self-contracted alliance of **Wardens** who serve under the same House. Unlike formal Clans, coalitions are **temporary, player-formed units** designed for tactical coordination, shared objectives, and social cohesion during a campaign.

> ‚ÄúA Coalition is not a command ‚Äî it is a pact. A promise made between Wardens, and to the House.‚Äù

## 2. Formation & Legitimacy

- A Coalition is **not considered formed** until it **signs a formal contract** with the House
- All members must be **active Wardens** of the same House at the time of signing
- The contract defines:
  - Shared objectives
  - Operational scope
  - Duration of service
- Coalitions without a House contract are **invalid and non-operational**

## 3. The Prizett Clause

- If a Warden‚Äôs personal service contract with the House expires **before** the Coalition‚Äôs contract ends, they may request a **Prizett**
- A **Prizett** is an **Imperial-era legal extension**, allowing the Warden to remain in service **for the duration of the Coalition‚Äôs contract**
- Prizetts are **granted at House discretion**, often based on merit, loyalty, or strategic necessity

## 5. Strategic Role

- Coalitions allow Houses to **scale operations** without rigid hierarchy
- Enable **expeditionary forces**, **Nexus incursions**, **Scanner defense rings**, or **cult suppression teams**
- Serve as **onboarding structures** for new players or **elite strike teams** for veterans

---

## 6. Political Risk and Clan Tensions

- Members may belong to **different Clans**, each with its own doctrine and priorities
- Coalition actions that conflict with Clan interests can lead to:
  - **Strained relations**
  - **Diplomatic fallout**
  - **Disciplinary action**, including expulsion

> ‚ÄúThe raid on Califos Gate was a tactical success ‚Äî and a political disaster. Three Clans withdrew support. One expelled its Warden.‚Äù
