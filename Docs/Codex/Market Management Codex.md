### 1. Core Principles

- **Fresh Campaign, Persistent Factions:**  
  - Each campaign begins with a reset economy.  
  - Factions, corporations, and players retain persistent assets, skills, and incomes.  
- **Faucets & Sinks:**  
  - **Faucets** inject credits, goods, and materials (production, contracts, events).  
  - **Sinks** remove them (maintenance, tariffs, consumption, corruption).  
- **Localized Economies:**  
  - Each House, station, and free port maintains its own market ledger.  
  - Arbitrage and station trading emerge naturally from regional price differences.  
- **Standing & Access:**  
  - Market access is gated by faction reputation.  
  - Angering a House or Corporation risks embargo or denial of docking rights.  
- **Neutral Hubs:**  
  - The Imperial Eye system functions as a free port with universal access.  
- **Corporations as Flex Points:**  
  - Neutral, cross‚Äëterritory contracts.  
  - Attractive for late‚Äëgame free traders seeking independence.

- **Buy/Sell Orders:** Posted by NPCs, players, factions.  
- **Matching Engine:** Orders execute when buy ‚â• sell price; partial fills allowed.  
- **Taxes & Fees:**

### 4. Underwriting & Contract Enforcement

- **Underwriters:** Corporations (and sometimes the USO) guarantee contracts.  
- **Imperial Enforcement:** Ensures contracts are honored, except underworld deals.  
- **Standing Benefits:**  
  - Lower fees, higher contract limits, faster enforcement.  
  - Some factions only accept contracts with trusted underwriters.  
- **Forgery Resistance:**  
  - High standing makes forged contracts less believable.  
  - Low standing makes actors easier to frame.

- **Pledging:** Contracts require standing pledges.  
- **Resolution:**  
  - Success ‚Üí pledge refunded, possible bonus.  
  - Failure ‚Üí pledge lost, bridges burned.  
- **Layered Contracts:**  
  - Multiple contracts (mining + defense + logistics) can be stacked.  
  - More secure, but more complex and costly.

### 6. Escrow Services

- **Workflow:** Deposit ‚Üí Verification ‚Üí Execution ‚Üí Release/Return.  
- **Types:**  
  - Imperial (high trust, high fee).

- **Espionage Surface:** Fees can be skimmed or embezzled, but assets remain secure.

### 7. Fee Manipulation & Embezzlement

- **Attack Surface:** Taxes, tariffs, escrow fees.  
- **Espionage Actions:**  
  - Skimming (low risk, low reward).  
  - Embezzlement (high risk, high reward).  
  - Forgery (frame another faction).  
- **Suspicion:**  
  - If anomalies detected, suspicion falls on both contract parties.  
  - Market Monitor audits fee flows.
