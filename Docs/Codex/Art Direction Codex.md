### Line Art Rules (Primary Focus)

- **Line Weight Hierarchy**: Use three core weights‚Äîthin for interior detail, medium for contours, thick for silhouettes and depth separation.
- **Decisive Strokes**: Lines should feel confident and economical; avoid excessive sketchiness except for intentional texture.
- **Cross-Contour Language**: Use cross-contour hatching on curved surfaces and cylindrical forms to describe curvature and surface flow.
- **Mechanical vs Organic Differentiation**: Prefer crisp, slightly angular strokes for hard edges and joins; use softer, tapered strokes for cloth and organic forms.
- **Silhouette Clarity**: Reinforce the outer silhouette with thicker outlines or deliberate shadow breaks to preserve immediate readability.
- **Seams and Panel Logic**: Draw seams, panel lines, and joins consistently with medium weight and add small bevel strokes where needed to imply chamfers.

---

### Shading and Rendering Rules (Primary Focus)

- **Block Tonal Planes First**: Establish core shadow, midtone, and key highlight before adding texture or detail.
- **Layered Hatching Strategy**: Model broad planes with parallel hatches; introduce cross-hatching at plane turns and occlusions; vary density to control value while keeping lines readable.
- **Hard vs Soft Transitions**: Render hard materials with sharp transitions and tight speculars; render soft materials with smoother gradients and softer edge halation.
- **Rim and Fill Lighting**: Use a narrow, crisp rim light to define edges and a broader, softer fill to model form; keep rim concentrated and fill diffuse.
- **Ambient Occlusion**: Emphasize gaps, creases, and overlaps with concentrated hatching or flat darks to anchor parts spatially.
- **Microtexture as Final Layer**: Apply scratches, rivets, woven patterns, and surface grain with fine hatch or stipple after primary form and value are established.

---

### Composition, Pose, and Detail Language

- **Framing and Silhouette**: Favor compositions that allow a clean, dominant silhouette; backlighting or strong directional light helps separate foreground from background.
- **Fidelity Focus**: Reserve highest line and shading fidelity for focal elements; simplify peripherals to avoid visual competition.
- **Scale and Overlap Cues**: Use overlapping elements and occlusion to convey scale while keeping the primary silhouette unambiguous.
- **Rhythm and Negative Space**: Maintain purposeful negative space around the primary subject to emphasize form and line clarity.
